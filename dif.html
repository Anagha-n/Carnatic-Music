Carnatic Music

In carnatic music there is always scope for improvisation as we could always add any kalpana swaras (which are imaginary swaras in the same raga as the song used to emphasise raga in the song. it can be added even in between the song.) Carnatic music has 5 different components, 2 of which are used even in western music.

  • Raagam or Melody
    This is a set of swaras which are combined together by certain rules, have particular prominent notes and patterns.
  • Taalam or Rhythm
    This is the rhythmic framework in Carnatic music which tells you where to start the note of the song. It is the external form of laya. There are different forms of taalams. They consist of fixed cycles called avarthanas.
  • Laya or Internal Rhythm
    Laya is the tempo of music used as reference for the singer. it is used only in carnatic music.
  • Shruti or Drone
    Which is the constant note during the song and helps the singer.
  • Monophonic
    This is specific to carnatic music, monophonic basically means that all the insuruments and the singer will be playing or singing the same tune.

Western Music

All Western classical music compositions are formally written using the staff notation, and performers have virtually no latitude for improvisation. But even western music has it's own good qualities it is scientifically proven to enhance cognitive performance, it is intricate and complex so people like listening to it. western music also has 4 components of which 2 are the same as carnatic music.

  • Melody
    This the product of many pitches in music when you move from, different pitch to pitch it becomes melody.
  • Polyphonic
    This is specific to western music, polyphonic basically means that there can/will be 2 different notes or chords playing at the same time.
  • Harmony
    Harmony is the sound of things that go together well — people singing in harmony are in tune with each other.
  • Rhythm
    The beat is the pulse of the music and tempo is how fast that pulse is moving. These are two parts of Rhythm in western music.
